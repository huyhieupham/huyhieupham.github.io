Huy Hieu Pham

I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. degree in Computer Science at the Toulouse Computer Science Research Institute (IRIT), France and Cerema Research Center. My research focuses on Computer Vision, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning. More specifically, I design and optimize high-performance deep learning networks such as Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (D-CNNs) for solving the security problems in public transport, e.g. the problem of human action recognition and behavior analysis in videos.

At Cerema and IRIT, I was advised by Louahdi KHOUDOUR, Alain CROUZIL, Pablo Zegers, and Sergio A. Velastin. From November 2015 to May 2016, I have done a research internship at the ICA laboratory, Ecole des Mines d'Albi, France. My work is part of a dynamic multi-partner robotized airplane inspection project, called Air-Cobot , lead by AKKA Technologies and AIRBUS group. The goal is to develop a vision-based method for detecting damages such as scratches or dents on the airplane exterior surface by processing 3D point cloud obtained from a 3D scanner. To this end, the Air-Cobot needs to move around the airplane and optically scan images with a mounted 3D scanner. These obtained point clouds will be processed by 3D image processing algorithms for detecting and characterizing of defects. Before that, I did my bachelors at the Hanoi University of Science and Technology (HUST).

Previously, I was a Computer Engineering undergraduate student at the Center for Training of Excellent Students, Hanoi University of Science and Technology (HUST). My research interests include Image Processing, Computer Vision and Machine Learning. I have done my graduate internship at International Research Institute MICA, Hanoi, Vietnam et AGIM laboratory, Université Grenoble Alpes, Grenoble, France. The main goal of my graduate project is to develop a computer vision algorithm based on electrode matrix and mobile Kinect for detecting obstacles and warning to visually impaired people.
